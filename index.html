I am an emerging AI Chip Architect and RISC-V SoC designer focused on building high-performance, energy-efficient AI accelerators using open-source silicon ecosystems. My work spans the full stack — from transistor-level CMOS physics to RTL design and system architecture.

I specialize in digital architecture and VLSI, with deep focus on arithmetic unit design — Wallace Tree multipliers, Carry Save Adder trees, fast adder families — and on timing analysis, propagation delay, and critical path optimization. I reason from transistor charging behavior all the way up to full-chip organization.

My trajectory is toward designing AI accelerators and full RISC-V SoCs using open-source EDA flows. I also work with embedded hardware (ESP32-S3, PCB design, UART/SWD debugging) — I am not only a theorist, but a builder.



My technical interests include AI accelerator architecture, RISC-V SoC design, arithmetic unit optimization, CMOS circuit-level reasoning, timing closure, hardware–software co-design, and open-source silicon (OpenLane / SkyWater PDK).
